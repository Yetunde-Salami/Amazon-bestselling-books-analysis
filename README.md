# Amazon Bestselling Books Analysis (2009–2019)

## 📊 Project Overview

This project analyzes Amazon’s bestselling books dataset from 2009 to 2019 to identify patterns in genre performance, pricing, ratings, and reader engagement.

The objective is to understand the factors that influence book success and how reader preferences differ across fiction and non-fiction categories.

---

## 🧠 Business Questions

* How do fiction and non-fiction books compare in popularity over time?
* Is there a difference in pricing between genres?
* How do ratings vary between fiction and non-fiction books?
* Which genre drives more reader engagement (reviews)?

---

## 🛠 Tools Used

* Microsoft Excel

  * Data cleaning
  * Pivot tables
  * Data visualization (charts)

---

## 📈 Key Insights

### 1. Genre Trends Over Time
![Genre Analysis](Genre%20Analysis.PNG)

Non-fiction consistently dominates the bestseller list across most years. However, in 2014, fiction briefly surpassed non-fiction, potentially influenced by external factors such as increased popularity of major book series and media adaptations.

---

### 2. Pricing Analysis
![Price Analysis](Price%20Analysis.PNG)

Non-fiction books have a significantly higher average price (14.84) compared to fiction (10.85), indicating that readers may be willing to pay a premium for practical and knowledge-based content.

Additionally, average book prices have declined in recent years, which may reflect evolving pricing strategies, increased competition, or improved accessibility in the market.

---

### 3. Ratings Analysis
![Ratings Analysis](Ratings%20Analysis.PNG)


Non-fiction books tend to receive slightly higher average ratings, suggesting strong perceived value and reader satisfaction for informational or solution-based content.

---

### 4. Reviews and Engagement
![Reviews Analysis](Reviews%20Analysis.PNG)

Fiction books generate a higher volume of reviews compared to non-fiction, indicating stronger reader engagement and interaction. This suggests that while non-fiction is valued for utility, fiction encourages discussion and emotional response.

---

## 📌 Conclusion

The analysis highlights a clear distinction between fiction and non-fiction markets. Non-fiction dominates in terms of frequency, pricing, and ratings, reflecting its value-driven nature. In contrast, fiction excels in reader engagement, emphasizing its role in entertainment and discussion.

These findings demonstrate how different types of content meet different reader needs, ultimately shaping their performance in the marketplace.
